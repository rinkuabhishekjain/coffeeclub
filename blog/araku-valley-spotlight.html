Araku Valley: India's Hidden Coffee Paradise

Discover the story of Araku Valley, where tribal farmers produce some of India's finest organic coffee in the Eastern Ghats of Andhra Pradesh.

By Priya Sharma February 14, 2026 12 min read

Nestled in the Eastern Ghats of Andhra Pradesh, Araku Valley is a testament to how traditional farming practices and modern quality standards can create exceptional coffee.

The Geography

At elevations between 900-1,100 meters above sea level, Araku Valley's unique microclimate creates ideal conditions for coffee cultivation with cool temperatures, abundant rainfall, rich soil, and natural shade.

A Story of Empowerment

What makes Araku truly special isn't just the coffee—it's the people behind it. Over 10,000 tribal farmers from the Adivasi community cultivate coffee in Araku. The Naandi Foundation's intervention in 1999 transformed subsistence farming into a thriving organic coffee industry.

Flavor Profile

Araku coffee is known for its distinctive characteristics:

  • Aroma: Floral with hints of chocolate
  • Body: Medium to full
  • Acidity: Bright but balanced
  • Flavor notes: Caramel, Dark chocolate, Citrus, Subtle spice

International Recognition

Araku coffee has won numerous awards including Gold Medal at Paris Coffee Festival (2017) and Prix Epicures OR award in France (2019).

Conclusion

Araku Valley represents the best of Indian coffee—exceptional quality, sustainable practices, and meaningful social impact. Every cup tells a story of transformation, from tribal farmers in remote hills to coffee lovers around the world.
